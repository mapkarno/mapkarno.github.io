Theme

Thematically, this book explores a young girl’s first love, which for many is a relationship between herself and an animal. Because an animal loves unconditionally, it gives a young girl the space to nurture another creature and open her loving heart without censor or insecurity.

When this dynamic is challenged by an animal’s dysfunctional relationship with reality, we enter into a more sophisticated telling of what it is to love and let go if love is not enough to heal the hurt behind the dysfunction. As girls mature into young women and discover first love with another human being, she will at some point encounter what it is to fall for someone who might not be right for her or might not be ready for her love. No matter what the circumstances, it is invaluable for any young girl or woman to learn that sometimes “love is not enough” and “tis better to have loved and lost than to never have loved at all.” ~ Alfred Lord Tennyson.
